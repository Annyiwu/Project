Formation

In general, gemstones are formed by crystallization of minerals under different conditions of temperature (heat), pressure and time. Gemstones, which have a mineral origin, come from rocks which themselves are made up of one or more igneous, sedimentary and metamorphic minerals. These three rock categories are in a continuous process of rock formation.

Igneous process

The solidification of magma from molten rock & associated fluids. The process starts from the eruption of magma from the mantle, and if the magmatic mass cools slowly in the crust before solidating into lava, it can crystallize and form minerals. Increasing pressure then causes chemical exchanges between this pegmatitic fluid with surrounding rocks, generating most of minerals found in the rocks including all quartz, beryl (emerald), garnet, moonstone, diamond, and etc.

Sedimentary

After the igneous rock reaches the surface of the earth, the forces of erosion and weathering produce smaller particles, and as time passes, the pressure between layers lead to various chemical and physical changes and create sedimentary rock. Gemstones associated with sedimentary rock include jasper, malachite, opal and zircon.

Metamorphism

The presence of intrusive magma in an area puts igneous and sedimentary rock and minerals under heat or pressure, causing changes in their chemistry and crystal structure. The creation of metamorphic rock includes gemstones such as beryl, jade, spinel, ruby, sapphire and zircon.
